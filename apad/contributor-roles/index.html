Investigating the division of scientific labor using the Contributor Roles Taxonomy (CRediT)

Paper

Vincent Larivière, David Pontille, Cassidy R. Sugimoto; Investigating the division of scientific labor using the Contributor Roles Taxonomy (CRediT). Quantitative Science Studies 2021; 2 (1): 111–128. doi: https://doi.org/10.1162/qss_a_00097

DATA

  1. CRediT Conceptualization, Data curation, Formal analysis, Funding acquisition, Investigation, Methodology, Project administration, Resources, Software, Supervision, Validation, Visualization, Writing-original draft, Writing-review & editing

  2. There was significant difference between the mean number of authors per paper of each journals. However, the mean numbers of contributions per paper are quite constant across journals. (10.6 in PLOS ONE) The mean number of authors of PLOS ONE is 6.8.

  • They iteratively focused on the first and last 2–5 characters of the names; this allowed us to match 221,637 authorships (99.4% of the sample). ==> this can be used in my project to match the editorship.

RESULT

  1. the percentage of papers on which each contribution appears.

  • Nearly all papers had an author writing th original draft, as well as authors reviewing and editing and concpetualizing them. (99 %)

  • The percentages of papers with at least one author contributing to formal analysis (91%), methodology (90 %), and investigation (86 %) are also very high, suggesting that empirical papers are the bulk of those published in these journals.

  1. Division of labor across authors
  • assess the percentage of authors who performed a given contribution.
  • the majority of authors contribute to writing—review and editing (68%), as well as methodology (55%), investigation (53%), and conceptualization (51%).
  • In the case of PLOS ONE, 68 % of authors contribute to Writing -Review & Editing. 55 % of authors contribute to Mehtodology and 52 % of peopel contribute to Investigation.
  1. Division of labor and number of authors
  • the percentage of authors who have performed a given task, for papers between 1 and 20 authors shows that as the number of authors increases, tasks are increasingly divided—although the extent to which they are varies as a function of the tasks involved.
  • while some tasks are performed by a smaller proportion of authors as the number of authors increases, other tasks remain relative stable once a certain threshold is met.
  • the proportion of authors who contribute to investigation and writing review and editing task stabilizes once 10 authors are reached, with, again, about half of authors contributing to the task.
  • Other tasks, however, are increasingly divided as the number of authors increases
  • Stable point is 6~7 in investigation.
